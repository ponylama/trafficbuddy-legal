TrafficBuddy – Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 18, 2026

TrafficBuddy ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring a secure mobile experience. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you utilize the TrafficBuddy mobile application (the "Application" or "Service").

Please read this policy carefully. By installing, accessing, or using the Application, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with the terms of this Privacy Policy, please do not access or use the Application.

1. Information We Collect and How We Process It

We distinguish strictly between data that is processed locally on your device and data that is transmitted to our servers.

A. Information Transmitted to and Processed on Our Servers

To provide the core functionality of the Service, we must collect and securely transmit the following identifiers:

  • Account Authentication (Phone Number): We collect your mobile phone number solely to establish, verify, and secure your account identity. Authentication is managed securely via Google Firebase Authentication.
  • Profile Data (Display Name): We collect the display name you choose during registration to identify you to your approved contacts within the Application.
  • Device Identifiers (FCM Tokens): We collect your Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) token to facilitate real-time push notifications when your authorized connections are driving.
  • User-Initiated Contact Syncing: When you choose to add connections, the Application accesses your device's address book locally. Only the specific phone numbers you explicitly select are transmitted to our servers to verify mutual contact status. We do not upload, store, or map your entire contact list.

B. Information Processed Exclusively On-Device (Never Transmitted)

To ensure maximum user privacy, the following highly sensitive telemetry data is processed strictly within your device's local runtime environment and is never transmitted to, or stored on, our external servers:

  • Location and Speed Data (GPS): The Application monitors your device's GPS speed metrics locally to automatically detect the initiation and termination of driving states. Your precise geographic coordinates are never tracked, transmitted, or logged.
  • Bluetooth Connectivity Telemetry: The Application reads local Bluetooth device names and MAC addresses exclusively to determine when your device connects to a designated vehicular system. This metadata remains restricted to the local device environment.

C. Automated Diagnostic Analytics

  • Crash and Performance Data: We utilize Google Firebase Crashlytics to collect anonymized technical diagnostic logs, stack traces, and crash reports to maintain application stability and debug system errors.

2. Purposes of Data Processing

We process your data strictly under the following legal and operational bases:

  • To Deliver the Core Service: To programmatically detect driving states and seamlessly notify your mutually approved connections.
  • Account Security: To securely authenticate users and mitigate unauthorized account access.
  • System Reliability: To analyze technical crashes and optimize application performance.
  • Communications: To dispatch essential transactional push notifications directly related to the Service's functionality.

3. Data Sharing and Third-Party Disclosures

We do not sell, lease, trade, or rent your personal data to third parties. Your data is disclosed to third-party service providers solely to the extent necessary to host, operate, and secure the Application.

Infrastructure Service Providers

  • Google Firebase (Alphabet Inc.): We rely on Google Firebase for critical infrastructure, including user authentication, push notification routing (FCM), and diagnostic logging (Crashlytics). All such data processing is governed by the Google Partner Privacy Policy.

We may also disclose your information if required to do so by applicable law, court order, or lawful government request.

4. Data Security and Retention

Data Security

We implement industry-standard administrative, technical, and physical security measures—including transport-layer encryption (SSL/TLS)—to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, or disclosure. However, please be advised that no method of digital transmission or storage can be guaranteed 100% secure.

Data Retention

  • Server-Side Data: Your account data (phone number, display name, and active tokens) is retained exclusively for the duration of your active account lifecycle.
  • Local Telemetry: As noted, location, speed, and Bluetooth identifiers are processed transiently on-device and are not retained systematically.

5. User Rights and Data Deletion

You maintain full control over your personal data. Depending on your jurisdiction, you have the right to access, correct, or permanently erase your personal data.

Account and Data Erasure: You may request the immediate and permanent deletion of your account and all associated server-side data at any time. To execute a deletion request, please contact our privacy compliance desk at the email address provided in Section 8. Upon verification, your data will be purged from our active databases in accordance with regulatory timelines.

6. Children's Privacy

The Application is designed for licensed drivers and is not directed at or intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect, maintain, or process personal data from individuals under 13 years of age. If we discover that a child under 13 has provided us with personal data, we will take immediate steps to delete such information from our systems.

7. Compliance Updates and Modifications

We reserve the right to amend this Privacy Policy at our discretion to reflect changes in our operational architecture, security practices, or global privacy regulations. Any updates will be signaled by a revision of the "Last Updated" date at the top of this document. Significant changes will be communicated via prominent notice within the Application interface.
